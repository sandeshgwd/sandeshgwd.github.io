My journey through science has grown from building electronic hardware systems in high school to now working in the robotics industry. Currently I work with Caterpillar on self-driving mining trucks; expertise in path and motion planning.

I landed here under the self-driving division while working on landmine detection problem for my thesis. My work has been linked for my passion towards mobile robots. I worked on various robots youbot, baxter, Labo3 - neptune customized for the thesis, Husky A200 during my Masters program for controls, path planning and motion planning.

I am an alumni of Next Generation Systems (NGS) Lab from University of Texas at Arlington (UTA) and worked under the guidance of Dr. Dan Popa.
