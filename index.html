Path - planning engineer with hands-on implementation and designing of autonomous system modules for complete automation of mining trucks working with Caterpillar. Supported successful deployment of driver-less mining trucks in production over different geographical continents.

I graduated from the University of Texas at Arlington (UTA) with my masters in Electrical Engineering and am an alumni of Next Generation Systems lab at UTA. Defended my thesis in May-15 on Autonomous Robotic Landmine detection, mapping and avoidance under the guidance of Dr. Dan Popa.

My thesis was initiated with a passion to work on mobile robots and a project offering the chance to automate the removal of mines in future. Represented UTA (Team ORION) for the Humanitarian Robotics and Automation Challenge (HRATC) at the International Conference of Robotics and Automation (ICRA). We won at Hong Kong in June'14 and led the team to the RUNNER UP position in June'15.
